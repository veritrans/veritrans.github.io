Introduction to VT-Web

How it works

Simple flow explanation

  1. Customer finish order and click checkout button on the merchant website.
  2. Customer redirected to VT-Web payment page on Veritrans website.
  3. Customer fill the payment information (Credit card details, billing address, etc.) on the VT-Web payment page on Veritrans website.
  4. Customer see the payment process status (success, failed, or pending), and then redirected back to merchant website to follow the next instruction from merchant.
  5. Customer & merchant recieve payment status notification via email.

Detailed flow explanation

This is what happens behind the scene in a more technical view:

  1. Customer finish order and clicks checkout on the merchant website.
  2. Merchant server requests two tokens to VT-Web to be used for redirection.

  3. VT-Web will respond with two tokens: token_merchant and token_browser.

    token_merchant is used for verification purposes when providing notifications back to the merchant server.

    token_browser is used as one of the parameters to redirect the customer to the VT-Web payment page on Veritrans website.

  4. Utilizing the token_browser, the customer will be redirected to the VT-Web payment page on Veritrans website.
  5. Customer enters payment details and completes the transaction on VT-Web's payment page on Veritrans website.
  6. After the payment process is complete, the customer will be redirected back to a page designated by the merchant.
  7. At the same time, Veritrans will send an email notification to the merchant admin and the customer.
  8. Veritrans will also send a HTTP(S) POST request as notification to the merchant server. This HTTP(S) POST request consists of payment status information. With this information, merchant server can update the transaction status in the merchant database (For example: success, failure, challenge).
  9. Payment process is complete.
